Amboseli National Park and Tsavo East from Nairobi.


ITINERARY


Day 1 : Nairobi - Amboseli National Park

Day 1 Nairobi–Amboseli National Park. Departure from Nairobi for Amboseli National Park, arrival at the lodge, installation, lunch and short rest then safari in Amboseli National Park from 3:30 p.m. Amboseli is one of the oldest reserves in Kenya, renowned for its location opposite Kilimanjaro (below) and its beautiful herds of elephants, but there are also lions, cheetahs, giraffes, hippo etc.!! and return to the lodge for the night. Accommodation at the Lodge or Camp on full board. The very famous Amboseli Park 400 km2 is one of the most beautiful parks in Kenya, with Kilimanjaro in the background (to see it, you have to get up early (or sometimes in the evening) because many clouds hide it from more often all day long. You will be able to observe many animals (zebras, wildebeest, giraffes, cheetahs, lions, etc.) Located 200 km south of Nairobi. Vast plain dotted with acacias, savannah, clear water swamps from the resurgence of Kilimanjaro it is here that Ernest Hemingway wrote “The Snows of Kilimanjaro” and Joseph Kessel “The Lion”.

Day 2 : Amboseli National Park - Tsavo East National Park

Departure after breakfast for a safari in Amboseli National Park, then head towards Tsavo East National Park. The semi-arid plains of East Tsavo extend from the Taita Hills to the Yatta escarpment where the broad Galana River flows and where large numbers of hippos and crocodiles live. Herds of elephants as well as herds of buffalo are numerous on the vast plains of Tsavo-East. Tsavo East also has the particularity of hosting numerous groups of lions, cheetahs safari in the Tsavo East park, then lunch and rest at the lodge until 4 p.m. then afternoon safari until 6 p.m.: Dinner and night at the lodge or camp on full board. Tsavo East National Park is one of the oldest parks in Kenya. The two national parks Tsavo East and Tsavo West together form one of the largest nature reserves in Africa (21,000 km2). Tsavo as a whole has an area of ​​10 million hectares, within which there are savannah, mountain ranges and hills, acacia forests and an extensive hydrographic network.

Day 3 : Tsavo East National Park - Diani/Mombasa or Malindi

Morning after breakfast game drives in Tsavo East National Park in search of lions, cheetah, antelopes, elephants with many other animals and leave the park for the direction of Diani, Mombasa or Watamu at your hotel arrived in the afternoon…end of safari.
