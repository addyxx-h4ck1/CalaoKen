Samburu, Aberdaires, Baringo, Bogoria, Amboseli, Tsavo West and Tsavo East


ITINERARY


Day 1 : Nairobi-The Samburu Reserve

Your Kenya safari adventure begins with a 6am pickup from the airport. Briefing, then departure for the Samburu reserve. Lunch en route. Arrival in Samburu in the afternoon with safari en route. Installation, dinner and night at the Lodge or Camp

Day 2/3 : Samburu and Bufalo Springs full day safari

Photo safari in the Samburu and Buffalo Springs reserves after breakfast at the lodge, and return to the lodge for lunch then further safaris in the afternoon until sunset. Dinner and night at the Camp lodge

Day 4: Samburu - Aberdaires National Park

Departure for the Aberdaires National Park, arrival at the Aberdaires Country Club for lunch then transfer to The Ark Lodge, famous for wildlife observation, is the best than any other establishment in this park due to corrosion. Afternoon photo safari then return to the Lodge. Your accommodation is located in the heart of the Aberdaires National Park. The Ark Lodge overlooks a floodlit waterhole and salt lick, which attracts crowds of impressive wildlife. Resembling and named after Noah's Ark, it consists of three decks from which numerous balconies and lounges provide a superb location for wildlife. To ensure you don't miss any animal sightings, the night watchman will buzz your room when exceptional animals show up at the watering hole.

Day 5: Aberdaires – Lake Baringo

After breakfast, drive to Lake Baringo. It is one of the freshwater lakes of the Rift Valley and a paradise for bird watchers. It has over 460 recorded bird species and a number of hippos. The installation at Tumbili cliff Camp, whose rooms contain a lot of information on birds. A resident birder leads walks before breakfast and in the evening. Dinner and night at the lodge.

Day 6: Lake Baringo

After breakfast take a 1 hour bird watching boat trip to see more birds and hippos then defer and have lunch, After lunch proceed to Bogoria Lake. Lake Bogoria is a shallow soda lake that is home to thousands of flamingos, large numbers of birds and the lesser kudu. Scalding hot springs and geysers at the southern tip of the lake gush and choke their alkaline waters. The pink flamingos feeding in the bluish water present a rare beauty. Dinner and night at the lodge

Day 7: Lake Baringo-Amboseli

Departure in the morning after breakfast, drive towards Amboseli National Park, arrival, installation at the lodge then rest, dinner and overnight. Accommodation at the lodge or camp.

Day 8: Amboseli Full day

Day dedicated to the exploration of Amboseli. Departure at dawn to bypass the Enkongo marshes permanently fed by the snows of Kilimanjaro where the herds of elephants concentrate, passage to the edge of the OlTukai palm forest where the lions hide. With picnic lunch in Amboseli National Park at 4 p.m. then return to the lodge or camp for dinner around a campfire and night under the tent and the African starry sky. Accommodation at lodge or camp

Day 9: Amboseli-Tsavo West.

Departure after breakfast for Tsavo West. Arrival for lunch at Rhino Vallé Lodge. Departure for a visit to the reserve in the middle of the afternoon until dusk. Visit to the Mzima Spring passage; resurgence of scrystalline rivers filtered in the volcanic rocks of Tsavo, forming a body of water inhabited by hippos and crocodiles. Return for dinner and overnight at the lodge.

Day 10: Tsavo West-Tsavo East

In the morning after breakfast, safari in Tsavo West, then departure for Tsavo East park. The semi-arid plains of Tsavo-East extend from the Taita Hills to the Yatta escarpment where the broad Galana River flows and where large numbers of hippos and crocodiles live. Herds of elephants as well as herds of buffalo are numerous on the vast plains of Tsavo-East. Tsavo East also has the particularity of hosting numerous groups of lions, cheetahs also like the plains of Tsavo East. Arrival at Tsavo East Park photo safari going to the lodge for installation, rest and afternoon safari until sunset. Tsavo East and Tsavo West together form one of the largest nature reserves in Africa (21,000 km2). Tsavo as a whole has an area of ​​10 million hectares, within which there are savannah, mountain ranges and hills, acacia forests and an extensive hydrographic network. Accommodation at lodge or camp

Day 11: Tsavo East-Diani

(5 hours connection): Departure after breakfast to search for animals in the park until the exit of Tsavo East park, leave to head towards Mombasa or Nairobi to your hotel or the airport. End of safari
